MESSAGE FROM THE HONORABLE VICE CHANCELLOR

Moot court is an activity that strives to provide young, aspiring attorneys with a taste of what the world of advocacy has in store for them. It teaches participants not only how to construct solid arguments based on significant research, but also how to deliver them in the most creative and confident way possible. It enables law students to comprehend the complexities of diverse sectors of law and the approaches to deal with them in greater depth. Competition law is one such field whose development is closely linked to that of the country. The discipline of competition law, which involves the study of competitive dynamics in the market, has assumed paramount importance in the dynamic economy of India, and has become an area teeming with new and exciting potential. The frontiers of competition law increase with each step the world takes toward globalisation. In order for India to become a leader in the post-COVID world, the next generation of Indian lawyers must possess all of the skills necessary to flourish in a field as important as competition law. Moot courts, arguably the greatest conceivable way for students to learn the workings of antitrust law and other subjects of law, have been made a core part of National Law University, Jodhpur's pedagogy in pursuit of this precise purpose. In terms of moot court competitions, the annual Antitrust Law Moot Court Competition is one of the most significant achievements of National Law University, Jodhpur in this sector, in addition to its excellent record of results in leading national and international moots. The Antitrust Moot, now in its thirteenth year, provides an excellent platform for students from law schools all over the country to understand the workings of law inside a courtroom by enabling them to argue before antitrust lawyers and academicians, including judges from India's two most powerful competition authorities, the Competition Commission of India and Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas. The NLU Antitrust Moot Court Competition has earned a reputation as one of the country's most prominent moot court competitions throughout the years. The Antitrust Moot Court Competition stands out every year in terms of the experience it delivers to its competitors. Even the coronavirus pandemic couldn't stop the Antitrust Moot Court Competition from continuing its march forward, with this year's edition being held fully online. The key to the Antitrust Moot Court Competition's success is the organisers' solemn dedication in providing the conducive environment for the nurturing the future legal brains, as well as their never-ending quest to push the boundaries of human knowledge in the field of law. I applaud the National Law University, Jodhpur's entire team for successfully organising the Thirteenth NLU Antitrust Law Moot Court Competition this year. The students, instructors, and staff participating in the competition's organisation did not let the ongoing pandemic dampen their spirits, which is a source of great pride for the entire National Law Universities community. Rather, in true budding lawyer flair, the organisers devised inventive ways to ensure that even the constraints of an online event would not stifle the Competition's growth and that the Antitrust Moot, 2022 emerged as the best version of itself. I wish the competing teams the best of luck and hope that what they've learned during this competition will help them succeed in the future.

COMPETITION COMMISSION OF INDIA

The Competition Commission of India has been established to regulate competition in the market, to prevent any adverse effects on the consumers, and to promote economic growth in the country. It ensures that there are no unfair practices in the market that have a negative impact on healthy competition. One of its mandates is to partake in efforts with various stake holders to promote competition advocacy, create public awareness and 3 impart training on issues pertaining to competition law. As a part of its competition law advocacy initiatives, it has associated itself with National Law University, Jodhpur in the organization of the NLU Antitrust Law Moot Court Competition. The Commission’s support has been unwavering since the inception of the Moot in 2010. This association has created awareness of the work profile of the Competition Commission of India amongst students from various law schools across the country.

CYRIL AMARCHAND MANGALDAS

Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas (“The Firm”) is the largest full-service law firm in India, with over 750 lawyers, including around 130 partners, and offices in India’s key business centres – Mumbai, New Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad, and Chennai. Founded on May 11, 2015, the firm traces its professional lineage back to 1917, and the establishment of Amarchand & Mangaldas & Suresh A. Shroff & Co. (AMSS). Following considerable growth in the practice over the course of the century, Mr. Suresh Amarchand Shroff led a period of consolidation through the 1970s and 1980s, securing the firm’s preeminent role in India’s legal sector. This was followed by a further period of exponential growth, including the launch of several offices. During this time, in 1994, Mr. Cyril Suresh Shroff took over as the Managing Partner of the Mumbai region of AMSS, helping to build a firm of unparalleled experience and reputation in the Indian legal fraternity. The Firm is set up on the foundation of this outstanding legal practice. It embraces this legacy, while also bringing its partners and associates together with a new blueprint for the future, based on a strong clientfocused value system, a collaborative work culture and a pragmatic and solution-oriented approach to problem-solving. The Firm takes pride in its ability to support a large and varied client base including domestic and foreign commercial enterprises, financial institutions, private equity funds, venture capital funds, start-ups, and governmental and regulatory bodies. The Firm has been consistently providing timely, innovative, integrated, relevant and solution-oriented legal advice, which is also cost-effective. The people who have been associated with the Firm are renowned for maintaining honesty, fairness, integrity, and professionalism in everything they do.

EASTERN BOOK COMPANY & SCC ONLINE

Established in 1942, the Eastern Book Company (“EBC”) Group is the intellectual giant in Indian law publishing, with offices in several Indian cities. EBC publishes a wide range of legal commentaries, student texts, law reports and digests, and its products include pioneering works both in the print and electronic medium. With a list of over 550 authoritative and well-known titles, it is well recognized for its contribution to legal literature worldwide. Enterprise and innovation imbue each new publication. Eastern Book Company’s products have been a hallmark for quality, reliability and reasonable price. Not only is perfection desirable in itself but what is keenly felt is a desire to give the legal fraternity works of the highest standard and dependability. Using the finest of technological innovations, EBC has pioneered law information databases for making law easily accessible in the electronic medium. This has appeared in a revolutionary form, in the electronic medium, as SCC Online’s Supreme Court Case Finder (SCC Online). This Information Database and search program is a proven source for quick retrieval of case-law precedents of the Supreme Court of India.
